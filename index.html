Designed Vocalizations Dataset

Sound-Effect-Modified Human and Animal Voices for Non-Natural Voice Conversion

Anonymous submissions to Interspeech 2025
Advancements in AI-based voice conversion have revolutionized media applications, including films, audiobooks, and games. While research has primarily focused on natural human speech, artificially designed voices, such as monster growls and robotic sounds, remain underexplored despite their distinct acoustic properties and the complexities introduced by sound designers' intricate crafting. Hence, we introduce a novel dataset generated using the vocal effects processor “Dehumaniser,” comprising original audio and its variations with applied effects on speech and animal sounds. We constructed test sets using both synthesized and real-world sound-designed audio to evaluate model generalization. Experimental results highlight the potential of voice conversion models for timbre conversion in designed sounds, emphasizing the influence of sound effects on conversion quality and the need for further analysis.

Timbre Conversion Samples

These timbre conversion samples showcase the results of FreeVC and an enhanced model across various test scenarios, including Seen-to-Seen, Seen-to-Unseen, Unseen-to-Seen, Unseen-to-Unseen, and Zeroshot cases. This evaluation setup helps assess how well voice conversion models adapt to different sound effects and real-world sound-designed data.
